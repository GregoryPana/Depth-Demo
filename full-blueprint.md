PAGE 3 — TRY A DIVE (DISCOVER SCUBA)
Purpose: Convert curious, nervous first-timers. Reassure. Excite. Remove every barrier.
Headline: You don't need experience. You just need to show up.
Intro:
The Discover Scuba Diving experience is designed for one purpose — to give you your first breath underwater in the safest, most supported environment possible. No prior experience required. No certification needed. Just curiosity and a willingness to try something that very few people ever forget.
What happens on the day:
Your morning begins at our dive centre in Mahé, where you'll meet your personal instructor. Before you enter the water, we walk through everything together — how the equipment works, how to breathe, how to equalise the pressure in your ears, and the simple hand signals we use underwater to communicate. It takes about 30 minutes and covers everything you need to feel confident.
We then move to our purpose-built training pool for a shallow confined water session. This is where you practice breathing underwater for the first time, in just a few metres of water, with your instructor beside you at all times. Most people are smiling within minutes.
Once you and your instructor are happy, we take you out on the boat for your open water dive in the Indian Ocean. Conditions off Mahé are warm, calm, and welcoming. You will descend to a maximum of 12 metres alongside your instructor, who stays with you throughout. The reef, the fish, the granite boulders — all of it waiting, just beneath the surface.

Duration: Approximately half day including pool session and boat dive
Minimum age: 10 years

PAGE 6 — OPEN WATER DIVER
Headline: Your first certification. Your diving life begins here.
What it is:
The PADI Open Water Diver course is the most recognised scuba diving certification in the world. Complete it with us in Seychelles, and you will be qualified to dive to 18 metres with a certified buddy anywhere on the planet — for the rest of your life.
How it works:
The course has three components, all of which we deliver in full:
Knowledge Development: The theory behind diving — physics, physiology, equipment, and dive planning. Available as PADI eLearning, which you complete online before you arrive and at your own pace. Or we run classroom sessions on site.
Confined Water Dives: Five skill sessions in our training pool. This is where you master the fundamentals — buoyancy, mask clearing, regulator recovery, emergency procedures. Done properly, this is what makes the rest feel effortless.
Open Water Dives: Four ocean dives across two days off the coast of Mahé. Each dive builds on the last, from shallow reef exploration to deeper dives approaching 18 metres. Your instructor is with you throughout.
Duration: 3–4 days depending on eLearning completion
Minimum age: 10 years (Junior Open Water), 15 years (full Open Water)
Prerequisites: Basic swimming ability. No prior dive experience needed.

PAGE 13 — SHARK BANK (INDIVIDUAL DIVE SITE)
Headline: The most talked-about dive in Seychelles. For good reason.
Depth: 25–45 metres
Level: Advanced Open Water and above · Long range · Conditions dependent
What you'll see: Grey reef sharks, white-tip reef sharks, nurse sharks, eagle rays, schools of snapper and barracuda, giant grouper. Seasonal: whale sharks from October.
The dive:
Shark Bank sits approximately 20km northwest of Mahé — a submerged plateau rising from the deep to around 25 metres, surrounded by open water dropping to over 200m. The currents that sweep across this plateau carry nutrients that attract everything that feeds on them — and everything that feeds on those things.
This is big animal diving. The sharks are not aggressive — they are present and unconcerned, which is exactly as it should be. You descend to the plateau, move slowly, and observe. On a good day, you may count twenty reef sharks at one time circling the rock formations. Eagle rays cruise past at depth. Schools of yellowfin snapper part around you.
If you have one dive left in Seychelles, this is the one.
Conditions: Best May–October during the southeast season · Not accessible in rough weather · Supplement applies for long-range travel

PAGE 14 — ENNERDALE WRECK (INDIVIDUAL DIVE SITE)
Headline: A sunken oil tanker. Thirty metres down. Completely colonised by the reef.
Depth: 20–32 metres
Level: Advanced Open Water and above · Long range
What you'll see: Massive schools of glassfish, moray eels, stonefish, batfish, lionfish, barracuda, soft corals covering every surface of the hull.
The dive:
The MV Ennerdale was a British naval fuel tanker that ran aground on a reef north of Mahé in 1970. In over 50 years on the seabed, the wreck has become one of the most densely populated artificial reefs in the Indian Ocean. The hull lies at around 30 metres — large enough to explore in sections across multiple dives.
Visibility inside the wreck sections is limited. We treat this as a penetration dive only for Wreck Diver certified guests — for all others, the exterior of the wreck provides more than enough to keep you occupied for the entire dive. The sheer density of marine life on and around the structure is astonishing.

PAGE 16 — SAINTE ANNE MARINE PARK DAY TRIP
Headline: The best day you'll have in Seychelles. No exaggeration.

Sainte Anne Marine National Park — established in 1973 as the first Marine Protected Area in the western Indian Ocean — sits just 5km from the coast of Mahé. Six islands, hundreds of species of marine life, and a day itinerary that fits everything in without ever feeling rushed.
This is our most popular tour. If you do one thing with us, make it this.

Board our glass-bottom boat and set off across the calm waters toward the marine park. From the moment you're moving, the underwater world is visible through the glass floor — coral formations, tropical fish, the occasional ray passing beneath.
Snorkelling Stop — Sainte Anne Channel
We anchor at one of the park's premier snorkelling sites — a coral garden home to over 150 species of fish. Equipment provided. Your guide enters the water with you and points out the highlights — parrotfish, oriental sweetlips, hawksbill turtles, moray eels. 45 minutes to an hour in the water.

The boat docks at Moyenne — a 9.9-hectare island turned wildlife sanctuary by one extraordinary man, Brendon Grimshaw, who spent 50 years transforming it from a barren patch of land into a thriving ecosystem. Jungle trails, pirate graves, and over 100 giant Aldabra tortoises roaming freely. An hour of wandering that feels entirely other-worldly.
Traditional Seychelles Cocktail
Back on board for the return cruise. A traditional Seychelles cocktail — coconut-based, cold, entirely appropriate.
4:30–5:00pm — Return to Eden Island Marina

PAGE 30 — CONSERVATION
Headline: We are guests in this ocean. We act like it.
Intro:
Every dive, every boat trip, every jet ski ride takes place in an ecosystem that has existed for millions of years and that we have the capacity to damage irreversibly in a single generation. We are not willing to be part of that story.
Since our founding, conservation has been woven into how we operate — not as a marketing position, but as a practical, daily commitment.
What we do:
Reef Monitoring: Our divemasters conduct regular coral health surveys at the sites we visit. Data is shared with the Seychelles Marine Conservation Society and contributes to long-term reef health tracking.
Responsible Dive Practice: We teach reef etiquette to every diver from day one. No touching. No feeding. No souvenirs. No standing on coral. This is not optional.
Reef-Safe Sunscreen: We provide reef-safe sunscreen on all boats and ask every guest to use it. Chemical sunscreens are among the primary contributors to coral bleaching.
Turtle Monitoring: We work with local turtle conservation programmes, recording sightings and nesting activity from our boats and dive sites. Hawksbill and green turtle numbers around Mahé have shown positive trends in recent years — we intend to keep it that way.
Marine Debris: Our crew collects any marine debris spotted on every trip. We participate in quarterly organised coastal and underwater clean-up events — guests are always welcome to join.
Whale Shark Code of Conduct: During whale shark season (October–November), we follow a strict code — no closer than 3 metres, no touching, no blocking the animal's path, limited time in the water. Whale sharks are the highlight of the season. We protect them.
Links:
Seychelles Marine Conservation Society · Association of Professional Divers Seychelles · PADI AWARE Foundation
